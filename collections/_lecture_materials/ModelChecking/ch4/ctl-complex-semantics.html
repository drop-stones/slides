--- title: CTL* Complex Semantics ---

In this page, we try to understand the semantics of complex CTL* formulas.
It is easy to understand if you break the formulas down into subformulas.

{% include_relative mermaid/ctl-example.html %} \[ \begin{array}[]\\ \pi_0 = \{ s_0, s_1, s_3, ... \} \\ \pi_1 = \{ s_0, s_1, s_4, ... \} \\ \pi_2 = \{ s_0, s_2, s_5, ... \} \\ \end{array} \]

\( M, s_0 \models \bm{EF} (b \land c) \)

Step.1: \( \bm{F} (b \land c) \)

\( \bm{F} (b \land c) = \{ \pi_0, \pi_1, ... \} \)
( This means \( \pi_0, \pi_1 \models \bm{F} (b \land c) \))

Step.2: \( \bm{E} \{ \pi_0, \pi_1, ... \} \)

\( \bm{E} \{ \pi_0, \pi_1, ... \} = \{ s_0, s_1, ... \} \)
( This means \( s_0, s_1 \models \bm{E} \{ \pi_0, \pi_1, ... \} = \bm{EF} (b \land c) \))

\( M, s_0 \models \bm{A} (b \bm{U} c) \)

Step.1: \( b \bm{U} c \)

\( b \bm{U} c = \{ \pi_0, \pi_1, \pi_2 \} \)

Step.2: \( \bm{A} \{ \pi_0, \pi_1, \pi_2 \} \)

\( \bm{A} \{ \pi_0, \pi_1, \pi_2 \} = \{ s_0, s_1, s_2, ... \} \)

\( M, s_0 \models \bm{EGX} b \)

Step.1: \( \bm{X} b \)

\( \bm{X} b = \{ \pi_0, \pi_1, ... \} \)

Step.2: \( \bm{GX} b \)

\( \bm{GX} b = \{ \pi_0, ... \} \)

  • \( \pi_0 \) holds \( b \) globally.
  • \( \pi_1 \) does not hold \( b \) globally.

Step.3: \( \bm{E} \{ \pi_0, ... \} \)

\( \bm{E} \{ \pi_0, ... \} = \{ s_0, ... \} \)

\( M, s_0 \not\models \bm{AFG} c \)

Step.1: \( \bm{G} c \)

\( \bm{G} c = \{ \pi_1^4, \pi_2^2, ... \} \)

Step.2: \( \bm{FG} c \)

\( \bm{FG} c = \{ \pi_1, \pi_2, \pi_1^4, \pi_2^2, ... \} \)

Step.3: \( \bm{AFG} c \)

\( M, s_0 \not\models \bm{AFG} c \)

  • \( s_0 \) have \( \{ \pi_0, \pi_1, \pi_2, ... \} \).
  • \( \pi_0 \) does not hold \( \bm{FG} c \).

Question: \( s_0 \, ? \, \bm{EXAX} (a \land b) \)

Step.1 \( \bm{X} (a \land b) = \{ \pi_0^1, ... \} \)
Step.2 \( \bm{A} \{ \pi_0^1, ... \} = \{ \} \)
Step.3 \( \bm{X} \{ \} = \{ \} \)
Step.4 \( \bm{E} \{ \} = \{ \} \)
So, \( s_0 \not\models \bm{EXAX} (a \land b) \)
